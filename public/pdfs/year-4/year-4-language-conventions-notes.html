üìå 1. Punctuation Marks

Full Stop (.) Question Mark (?) Exclamation Mark (!)

  • Full Stop: Ends a statement. The dog ran to the park.
  • Question Mark: Ends a question. Where is the library?
  • Exclamation: Shows strong emotion. Watch out!
Add punctuation: What time does the bus arrive __________

Comma (,)

Used for:

  • Items in a list (use before 'and' or 'or')
  • Introductory words (However, Therefore)
  • Joining two sentences with a conjunction
I bought apples, oranges, bananas, and grapes.
However, I forgot my wallet.
Add commas: However I was tired I kept studying.

Semicolon (;) and Colon (:)

Semicolon: Joins related clauses without conjunction

I wanted to go; however, it started raining.

Colon: Introduces a list or quotation

You need: pens, paper, and pencils.

Apostrophe (')

Possession:

  • Singular: boy's book, cat's toy
  • Plural: girls' room (ends in s)

Contractions: don't = do not | can't = cannot

The teacher's desk. | The teachers' room.
Write the full form: She'd = __________

Quotation Marks (" ")

Encloses spoken words. Periods and commas go INSIDE!

"Hello," said Mum. "How was your day?"
Tom exclaimed, "I won the game!"

üìñ 2. Grammar - Parts of Speech

Nouns

  • Common: General names (dog, city) - NOT capitalised
  • Proper: Specific names (Max, Sydney, Monday) - ALWAYS Capitalised
  • Concrete: Things you can touch (table, book)
  • Abstract: Ideas (happiness, courage)
Circle proper nouns: The students visited the museum on monday.

Verbs

  • Action: run, think, barks
  • Linking: is, are, was, were, seems
  • Helping: have, has, had, will, can
She has finished her homework.
Underline verbs: The birds are flying south for winter.

Adjectives and Adverbs

Adjectives: Describe nouns - what kind, how many, which one

Adverbs: Describe verbs - how, when, where, how much

  • Manner: quickly, slowly, carefully
  • Time: yesterday, tomorrow, soon
  • Place: here, there, outside
The children played happily outside.
Add adjectives: The __________ (1) dog chased the __________ (2) ball.

Subject-Verb Agreement

Verb must match the subject in number

SubjectVerb (+ s/es)Verb (no s)
He/She/Itwalks, reads-
I/You/We/They-walk, read
The cat chases the mouse. | The cats chase the mice.
Correct: The team (work/works) hard every day.

Tenses

Past: I walked, She went, They saw

Present: I walk, She goes, They see

Future: I will walk, She will go

Change to past: The bird flies south in winter.

Simple vs Compound vs Complex Sentences

  • Simple: One independent clause. The boy ran fast.
  • Compound: Two clauses + conjunction. The boy ran fast and he won.
  • Complex: Two clauses + because/although. The boy ran fast because he wanted to win.

üìù 4. Practice Questions

Punctuation

1. Add punctuation: What is your name __________

2. Add commas: However I was tired I kept studying.

3. Add commas: I bought apples oranges bananas and grapes.

Grammar - Multiple Choice

4. The team __________ (work/works) hard.

5. This book is __________ (your / you're).

6. The __________ (childrens / children) played in the park.

7. I __________ (hear / here) a strange noise.

Spelling - Correct the Spelling

8. She was very happy in her new home. ‚Üí happy ‚Üí __________

9. The teacher gave us an importent project. ‚Üí importent ‚Üí __________

10. We had a wonderfull day at the beach. ‚Üí wonderfull ‚Üí __________

Answers

1) ? 2) However, I was tired, I kept studying.

3) I bought apples, oranges, bananas, and grapes.

4) works 5) your 6) children 7) heard

8) happy 9) important 10) wonderful

üí™ NAPLAN Tips

  • Read carefully: Read the question twice before answering
  • Eliminate wrong answers: Cross out options you know are wrong
  • Check spelling: Read your answer backwards to catch errors
  • Verb agreement matters: Does the verb match the subject?
  • Punctuation marks matter: Periods, commas, and question marks change meaning
  • Practice daily: 15 minutes of language practice each day!
