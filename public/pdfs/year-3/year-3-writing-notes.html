üìñ 1. What is Narrative Writing?

Narrative = Story

A narrative tells a story with a beginning, middle, and end. It has characters, a setting, and a problem/solution.

Good narratives have: Characters + Setting + Problem + Solution = Great Story!

üèóÔ∏è 2. Story Structure

The Beginning

Introduce: Characters, Setting, and the Situation

"Once upon a time, in a small village near the forest, lived a brave young girl named Lily."
Write your beginning: Who is your character? Where are they?

The Middle (The Problem)

Include: A problem or challenge for your character

"One day, Lily discovered that the forest animals were sad. The evil witch had cast a spell."
What problem will your character face?

The End (The Solution)

Show: How the problem is solved

"Lily sang a beautiful song. The magic broke the witch's spell. Flowers bloomed everywhere!"
How does your character solve the problem?

Use Interesting Words

"The dog ran." ‚Üí "The energetic golden retriever sprinted joyfully across the meadow."

Use Different Sentence Starters

Instead of "The boy ran," try:
  • "Excitedly, the boy ran."
  • "As fast as lightning, the boy ran."
  • "The boy ran towards the finish line."

Add Details (Sensory Words)

  • See: bright, shiny, colorful
  • Hear: loud, quiet, whispering
  • Smell: sweet, fresh, stinky
  • Feel: soft, rough, smooth

üí™ NAPLAN Writing Tips

  • Plan first: Know your beginning, middle, and end
  • Use paragraphs: New paragraph for new idea
  • Check spelling: Read backwards to catch errors
  • Check punctuation: Full stops, capital letters
  • Make it interesting: Use adjectives and adverbs
