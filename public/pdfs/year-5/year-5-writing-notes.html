üìù 1. What is Persuasive Writing?

Purpose

Persuasive writing tries to convince the reader to agree with your opinion or take action.

State your opinion clearly. Support it with reasons and evidence!

Structure

  1. Introduction: Hook + Your opinion (thesis)
  2. Body Paragraph 1: Reason 1 + Evidence
  3. Body Paragraph 2: Reason 2 + Evidence
  4. Body Paragraph 3: Reason 3 + Evidence
  5. Conclusion: Restate opinion + Summary + Call to action

‚úçÔ∏è 2. Persuasive Language

Opinion Words

Use these to state your position:

  • I believe / I think / In my opinion
  • It is clear that / Obviously
  • People should / We must
"I believe that schools should have longer breaks because..."

Emotive Language

Words that create feelings:

  • Positive: amazing, wonderful, beneficial, essential
  • Negative: terrible, harmful, unfair, ridiculous
"This is an amazing opportunity!" vs "This is a good opportunity."

Facts vs Opinions

Fact: Can be proven true or false (The sky is blue.)

Opinion: What someone thinks (Blue is the best color.)

üèóÔ∏è 3. Paragraph Structure

TEEL Paragraph

  • Topic sentence - Main idea
  • Explanation - Explain your point
  • Evidence - Facts, examples, quotes
  • Link - Connect back to opinion
"Schools should have longer breaks [T]. This gives students time to recharge their energy [E]. Research shows that regular breaks improve concentration and learning [E]. Therefore, longer breaks will help students perform better in class [L]."

üí™ NAPLAN Tips

  • Plan your ideas first
  • State your opinion clearly in the introduction
  • Give 3 reasons with evidence
  • Use persuasive language
  • End with a strong conclusion
  • Check spelling and punctuation
